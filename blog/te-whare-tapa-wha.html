Wellbeing plan using Tā Mason Durie's Te Whare Tapa Whā model

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

My Wellbeing Plan based on Te Whare Tapa Whā
Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)
  • Working out at the gym during lunch break or after class 3 x a week
  • Going for a swim, run or cycling during the weekend 1 x a week
  • Eating fruits and vegatables every day
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)
  • Having dinner with my friends or flatmates 2 x a week at least
  • Having a movie or game night with my flatmats 1 x every two weeks
  • Joining the hiking group monthly hike
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)
  • Praying to God every morning
  • Practicing mindfulness every day before bed
  • Taking a counselling session every two weeks
  • Keeping exercise at least 2 x a week
  • Taking the medications every day
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)
  • Going for a walk or run in nearby park or nature
  • Joining the hiking group monthly hike
